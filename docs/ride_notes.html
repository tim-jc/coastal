From one of the best days to one of the very toughest. There was an ominous red sky as the day dawned and we headed off. The route was isolated - I had extra water strapped on the fork as we would be hours before the first shop. Limited tarmac options for staying near the coast so we had to take a few gravel tracks to join the dead end paved roads together. Elsey Estate the first of these. Stopped once we were off the estate to eat the hard boiled eggs Schalk had made in the kettle. Rain had been drizzling all morning, but started to pick up at this point. Stopped for lunch at Craobh Haven and took the opportunity to get some dry kit on. We were only 27 miles from Oban and feeling pleased that a difficult day was nearly done. The other major bit of gravel took us over Bealach na Gaoithe (the ‘pass of the winds’). A steep loose climb up with fog and howling wind on the top. Waited for Wyndy, and as we rolled on, he flatted. Too big a cut for tubeless to seal, so had to get a tube in there. He’d put his tubes right at the bottom of his bag so bad to unpack everything right there on the hill. We were all get seriously cold and needed to get riding asap. There was a tricky descent with lots of sharp stones on the other side which we negotiated very carefully, terrified of another puncture. A couple of miles later we hit tarmac again and with the rain lashing down absolutely hammered it to Oban desperate to get warm. Airbnb had a dryer and apart from a quick Tennants at the pub, and grabbing a takeaway curry, spent the night in recovering and getting our kit ready for Mull the next day.
